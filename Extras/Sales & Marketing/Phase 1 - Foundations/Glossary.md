# Glossary — Phase 1 (Foundations)

Quick reference for abbreviations and terms used across the Phase 1 notes.

- ABM — Account-Based Marketing: Targeting and engaging a defined list of high-value accounts.
- ACV — Annual Contract Value: Contract value normalized to 12 months.
- AE — Account Executive: Sales role responsible for owning opportunities and closing deals.
- AI — Artificial Intelligence: Software systems that perform tasks typically requiring human intelligence.
- A/B Test — Split test of two variants to compare performance (e.g., headline A vs headline B).
- "Aha" Moment — The point where a user first experiences core product value.
- CAC — Customer Acquisition Cost: Total cost to acquire a customer (marketing + sales) over a period divided by customers acquired.
- CS — Customer Success: Team/function focused on adoption, value realization, retention, and expansion after purchase.
- CTA — Call To Action: The action you want the user to take (e.g., "Start trial", "Book demo").
- CRM — Customer Relationship Management: System of record for leads, contacts, accounts, and opportunities (e.g., HubSpot, Pipedrive).
- GA4 — Google Analytics 4: Google’s web/app analytics platform.
- ICP — Ideal Customer Profile: The type of company/account that gets outsized value and where you win repeatedly.
- JTBD — Jobs-to-be-Done: Framework describing the progress customers seek and what they “hire” a product to accomplish.
- KPI — Key Performance Indicator: A quantifiable measure of performance toward a specific outcome.
- LTV — Lifetime Value: The total gross margin expected over the lifetime of a customer.
- MEDDIC — Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Identify pain, Champion: A B2B qualification framework.
- MLG — Marketing-Led Growth: Growth driven primarily by marketing channels and content.
- MQL — Marketing Qualified Lead: A lead that meets marketing’s threshold for sales follow-up.
- PLG — Product-Led Growth: Growth driven through the product experience (self-serve, trials, freemium).
- PQL — Product Qualified Lead: A user/account that has demonstrated value through in-product behavior.
- ROI — Return on Investment: (Gain from investment − Cost of investment) ÷ Cost of investment.
- SDR — Sales Development Representative: Role focused on prospecting, qualification, and setting meetings.
- SEO — Search Engine Optimization: Improving organic visibility and traffic from search engines.
- SLA — Service Level Agreement: A documented expectation (e.g., MQL response time < 1 hour).
- SLG — Sales-Led Growth: Growth driven by outbound sales and high-touch motions.
- SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market: The portion of the market you can realistically capture next.
- SQL (Lead) — Sales Qualified Lead: A lead accepted by sales as meeting criteria to open an opportunity.
- SQL (Language) — Structured Query Language: Language for querying relational databases.
- TAM — Total Addressable Market: Total market demand for a product or service.
- SAM — Serviceable Available Market: The part of TAM you can serve with your business model/offer.
- UTM — Urchin Tracking Module: URL parameters for tracking marketing attribution.
- USP — Unique Selling Proposition: The specific differentiator that meaningfully matters to buyers.
- VWO — Visual Website Optimizer: Tool for A/B testing and conversion rate optimization.
- SN — Sales Navigator: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, a prospecting and account research tool.
